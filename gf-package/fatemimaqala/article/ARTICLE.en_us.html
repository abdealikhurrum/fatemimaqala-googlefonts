Fatemi Maqala is a Naskh typeface for Lisan ud Dawat — the Arabic-script language of the Dawoodi Bohra community — and for general Arabic and Latin text. It is an original retracing and extension of the Bohra manuscript Naskh hand: the outlines were substantially redrawn, the curves regularized for screen rendering, and the family extended with a full Latin set so the two scripts can set together cleanly.

The design keeps the calligraphic modulation of the manuscript tradition — the weighted bowls, the tapering finials, the deliberate joins — while normalizing proportions and spacing for body text. Arabic shaping covers initial, medial, final and isolated forms with contextual marks and ligatures; mark positioning and diacritic stacking were tuned against a large vocalised-text corpus to reduce collisions in dense religious typesetting. The Latin companion is a quiet, low-contrast serif sized to sit comfortably beside the Arabic.
