Fatemi Maqala is an Arabic Naskh typeface for Lisan ud Dawat, the language of the Dawoodi Bohra community. It is an original retracing and extension of the manuscript naskh hand long used in the community’s scribal tradition—a low-contrast, warm, handwritten style distinct from formal typeset naskh.

The design preserves the signature letterforms of that hand—the looped kaf, the knotted heh, the gentle teeth and rounded bowls—while the digital implementation targets robust cross-platform use in documents, print (booklet sizes, 150–300 dpi) and on screen.

Fatemi Maqala also covers the additional letters needed for writing Lisan ud Dawat and Urdu, including the retroflexes, gaf, che and zhe.

The typeface revives a tradition that originated within Aljamea-tus-Saifiyah and the wider Dawoodi Bohra community; that origin is gratefully acknowledged.
